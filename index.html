Rare knowledge about christmas

During the 17th century, Puritans in England and colonial America banned Christmas celebrations, considering them unbiblical and overly indulgent. The ban in Boston lasted from 1659 to 1681, and anyone caught celebrating was fined!

The famous Christmas tune "Jingle Bells" was composed by James Lord Pierpont in 1857, but it was intended for Thanksgiving. Over time, its cheerful melody and festive imagery made it a Christmas classic.

The tradition of decorating Christmas trees originated in Germany in the 16th century. Devout Christians brought trees into their homes, and Martin Luther is credited with adding the first candles to a tree to mimic the twinkling stars.

Before Coca-Cola popularized the red-suited Santa in the 1930s, he was often depicted in various colors like blue, green, and brown. The modern image of Santa Claus as we know it today is largely due to Coca-Cola's advertising campaigns.
